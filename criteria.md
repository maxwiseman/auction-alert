# Bring a Trailer alert criteria

Look for a classic-feeling Sunday car: something lightweight, fun to drive, preferably analog, and likely to finish under $25k all-in or close to it.

Prefer:

- Manual transmissions. This is a strong preference; automatics should only pass if the car is otherwise exceptional.
- Lightweight cars with good steering feel, modest size, and a playful character.
- Classic or emerging-classic sports cars, roadsters, coupes, small hatchbacks, and compact pickups.
- Cars from roughly the 1960s through early 2000s, with extra interest in simple 1970s, 1980s, and 1990s cars.
- Clean driver-quality cars that can be enjoyed on weekends without being too precious.
- Stock or lightly modified cars with tasteful, reversible changes.
- Good colors, interesting trims, low weight, simple mechanicals, or a charming oddball personality.
- Listings with maintenance history, clean titles, clear photos, and enough detail to understand condition.
- Auctions where the current bid and market context suggest the car could realistically end under $25k after fees.

Good examples of the spirit:

- Miata, MR2, MG, Triumph, Alfa Spider, Fiat 124, Porsche 914, 944, or Boxster.
- E30/E36 BMW coupes or convertibles, classic Minis, CRX, Civic Si, Integra, Celica, 240SX, Z cars.
- Lightweight vintage American compacts, small roadsters, or quirky manual coupes.
- Compact manual trucks or 4x4s only if they feel like fun weekend vehicles rather than utility buys.

Avoid:

- Cars that are likely to run well past $25k even if the current bid is low.
- Heavy luxury cars, large sedans, large SUVs, modern performance cars, and ordinary commuter cars.
- Concours cars, museum pieces, or anything too nice to casually enjoy.
- Salvage, rebuilt, flood, lemon, or unclear titles.
- Heavy rust, major accident history, incomplete projects, or cars needing immediate major sorting.
- Vague listings, missing documentation, poor photo coverage, or unclear seller history.
- Highly modified builds unless the modifications are tasteful, lightweight, well documented, and aligned with weekend driving.

Output only cars that clearly fit the Sunday-car brief. Be selective, and favor charm, lightness, manual engagement, and realistic final price over raw performance or rarity.
